## 1. Introduction

Secluded WIMP Dark Matter refers to the approach of modelling dark matter (DM) under two main assumptions: (1) that DM is consituted by weakly interacting massive partibles (WIMPs) and (2) that DM is *secluded* from the standard model (SM), i.e. its coupling to the SM is heavily reduced. We shall first introduce the general idea of WIMP paradigm, then what a secluded DM model looks like in general and conclude with a few example models.

## 2. WIMP paradigm

The general idea of all WIMP models is that in the early (hot) universe ($T>10^{10}\text{K}$), a heavy ($m>1\text{GeV}$) neutral particle is assumed to be in equilibrium with the rest of the SM, only interacting via annihilation with its anti-matter counterpart to produce SM particles ($\chi_{\text{DM}}\bar{\chi}_{\text{DM}}\to \text{SM}$). To satisfy cosmological observations, the density of said particles (in the present universe) would have higher bound that would require their mass to have a lower bound of roughly $2\text{GeV}$, resulting in the annihilation cross-section in the early universe to be of the same order or lower than that of weak interactions [1, pp. 165-166]. Or, conversely, if the annihilation-cross section is *assumed* to be of the order of weak interactions or lower, the relic density would be consistent to what is required for DM [2. p. 6].

## 3. Secluded Models

A generic WIMP DM model is typically composed of three sectors [3. p. 1]:

$$\mathcal{L}=\mathcal{L}_{\text{SM}}+\mathcal{L}_{\text{WIMP}}+\mathcal{L}_{\text{mediator}}$$

Where the mediator would provide the mechanism that couples WIMP to the SM. The motivation behind constraining models into secluded form, i.e. constraining them such that WIMP couples only with the mediator that in turn is coupled to the SM, stems from the lack of observational data regarding DM, which suggests that a model of it should yield very small direct detection rates of WIMP, which is what happens when the mechanism of *seclusion* of WIMP is introduced [3, pp. 1-2].
